Yoga for
Aging Bodies

Yoga is a joyful and safe practice that can be used as a complimentary or integrative therapy for the aging population. Give yourself the ability to sustain your highest quality of living at any age or stage of life.

There are many health benefits in practicing yoga… including: improve balance, mental clarity, increase flexibility, maintain focus, minimize pain, relieve anxiety and depression and increase strength.

Kelly specializes in aging bodies. She holds a certificate for Integrative Yoga for Seniors from Duke Integrative Medicine. Duke University is one of the world’s leading medical research and patient care institutions transforming healthcare with integrative approaches for improved patient care.

Yoga for
Ailments

Yoga can be adapted and applied to practically any phase of disease, ailment, or recovery from injury, from diagnosis through treatment, survivorship or progression. Once you received your doctors approval to begin yoga, we can find the appropriate style of yoga that is suitable for you.

From breathing techniques (pranayama) to mindful meditation (Dhyana) to gentle physical movements (asana), we will work together to uncover your greatest potential in a safe and fun atmosphere.

Yoga as a form of treatment or therapy can help reduce pain levels, reduce stress, and build body awareness while maintaining physical mobility.

Why Private Yoga?
Perhaps, because…
• you have injuries or ailments that require special treatment
• you are beginner and shy to attend group classes
• you are unable to make it to regular class times
• you would like guided breathing or meditation along with mindful movements
• you would like direct, one-on-one guided yoga from specialized practitioner

About Me

This is how I founded Avatara Yoga & Integrative Health, A Yoga Health Centre!

I, Kelly Yee am a certified Yoga Instructor (YT250) with specialized training from Duke Integrative Medicine in yoga for seniors and those living with ailments such as cancer, osteoporosis, and chronic pain. Additionally I hold a BCom., MSc., and attending Simon Fraser University in Gerontology.

My love for healthy living inspires me to research health sciences, integrative medicines, and age-related illnesses where Yoga could be utilized to help the physical, mental, emotional and spiritual well-being of others.

With many years of practicing yoga and pilates… I was on a quest to find a healthy balance between physical movement, environmental integrity and spiritual well-being.

Moving across the country for love and personal growth, I discovered that Yoga is the healthy healing therapy I consistently came back to, for fitness, mindfulness, and connection to spirit. A sequence of events led me to meet an inspiring and motivating yoga teacher (Jason, Yoga Tree Studios). I was given the tools and knowledge to advance my practice, uncover my passion of life-long learning and discover my love for teaching.

After my very first teaching opportunity, I felt this joyful sensation I had never felt. The sensation lifted me to another level, right then I knew this is my path!

I returned to my home city to create this Yoga Health Centre! Where aging adults and people living with chronic ailments can use yoga as a form of ongoing treatment and therapy, which allows you to live your best quality life at any age or phase!
